thimblr.

The easiest way to preview your Tumblr themes.

What is Thimblr?

Thimblr lets you preview your Tumblr themes right from your favorite editor. It protects your fingers from tedious copy-pasting a theme template into Tumblr's customize tool.

PLEASE NOTICE: This is an early Alpha version!

Thimblr is still in a very early stage and a lot of the Tumblr theme functionality is missing or not working properly. It's not really meant for public use just now. But if you would like to contribute to this project in any way, head over to GitHub and have a look or contact me here!

How do I use it?

Here is a list of editors that support Thimblr and instructions how to use them:

Textmate

  1. Download this version of the Tumblr Themes Textmate bundle: Tumblr Themes Bundle.
  2. Copy it to ~/Library/Application Support/Textmate/Bundles.
  3. Open Textmate and reload your bundles (Bundles → Bundle Editor → Reload Bundles).
  4. Open your Tumblr theme template and select Bundles → Tumblr Themes → Preview with Thimblr.
  5. Optionally, you can specify a shell variable TUMBLR_USERNAME in the Textmate Preferences with your Tumblr username as the value. This way, you can preview the theme with your own data.

How does it work?

Thimblr does a bad impression of Tumblr's templating system. If you could imagine a terrible impression of Jerry Lewis, and Jerry Lewis was Tumblr's templating system, that's what Thimblr is. It's not foolproof, and it can be unpredictable, but it should give you a good approximation of what your theme will look like. It tries its best to conform to Tumblr's theme docs.

Support any editor

If you want to support your favorite editor, you just need to find a way to send a HTTP POST request to http://thimblr.heroku.com/preview with these parameters:

  • theme_code: the whole template code.
  • username (optional): the Tumblr username to preview themes with custom data.

Then display the response body as HTML.
